About the Game

FrogMog is a fast-paced 3D multiplayer battle royale where you play as a frog fighting for survival in a shrinking swamp arena. Leap, tongue-lash, and outmaneuver your opponents in chaotic matches of up to 20 players.

Built from the ground up in Godot 4, FrogMog combines tight platformer controls with unpredictable battle royale mechanics. Every match is different — power-ups spawn randomly, the terrain shifts, and only the craftiest frog survives.

Whether you're ambushing from a lily pad or dodging across quicksand, FrogMog delivers pure multiplayer chaos with a ribbit.

Dev Log

Welcome to FrogMog

TJ

We're thrilled to officially announce FrogMog — a 3D multiplayer battle royale game where frogs duke it out in an ever-shrinking swamp arena. This has been a passion project for us, and we're finally ready to share it with the world.

The Concept

The idea started simple: what if battle royale games had more personality? We wanted something that felt chaotic and fun from the moment you drop in. Frogs were the perfect fit — they leap, they're fast, they have tongues that can grab things. The mechanics practically write themselves.

Why Godot 4?

We chose Godot 4 as our engine for its open-source philosophy, excellent 3D capabilities, and the incredible community behind it. The new GDExtension system lets us optimize performance-critical code in C++ while keeping gameplay logic in GDScript for rapid iteration.

Multiplayer Chaos

FrogMog supports up to 20 players in a single match. The arena starts as a sprawling swamp with lily pads, logs, mud pits, and hidden power-ups. As the match progresses, the safe zone shrinks — forcing frogs into tighter and tighter confrontations. Last frog standing wins.

What's Next

We're deep in prototyping right now, nailing down the core movement and combat mechanics. Sign up below to get notified when we have playable builds ready for testing. We'd love to have you in the swamp.
